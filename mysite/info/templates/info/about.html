About Us

We at the babble team acknowledge the dangers a simple baby reveal party can create. This September, a wildfire that has already burned over 7,000 acres of land in California has been attributed to a gender reveal mishap. This is not the first baby-reveal tragedy either. Other gender reveal parties in the past have also led to disastrous results. In July 2019, a car used to release blue smoke burst into flames. In 2017, a gender reveal led to a wildfire that burned through 47,000 acres. They can even be deadly, as shown in 2019 when a gender reveal party device blew up and killed a grandmother. Parties to celebrate a newborn are typically well-intentioned, however, they clearly pose a threat to the safety of participants and locals in areas with a high risk of wildfires.

babble offers a safer and effective alternative to these parties. Our application will allow users to virtually invite guests to celebrate the coming of their new child, saving nearby forests and foliage from potential destruction.
